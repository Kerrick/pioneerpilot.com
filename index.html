  • Five Build-Around Blue Cards

    Published October 27, 2019 by Mrs. Mulligan

    Pioneer should be a sweet spot for brewing sweet decks around powerful or unique cards. Because it’s smaller than Modern your brews don’t have to compete with incredibly streamlined and brutal decks. But because it’s larger than standard, unique strategies tend to have more support cards available to make things run smoothly. In this article we look at five build-around blue cards that can inspire your next Pioneer brew. Read More

  • Mono-Blue Prison: A Primer

    Published October 25, 2019 by Dustin Hubbartt

    Mono Blue Prison is a deck from Shadows Over Innistrad Standard that has a unique play style: it durdles, takes extra turns, casts a bunch of spells that don’t seem to do much… and then it wins the game! Pioneer adds key cards that strengthen the core strategy of the deck and create additional “hard locks” that keep your opponent out of the game. The deck is fun to play, it can hold its own, and it’ll surprise (but not delight) your opponents. Read More

    Pioneer’s initial ban list only includes five cards: the fetch lands. What does this mean for building a manabase? In this article we explore the multi-color lands available in the format, the best specialty mono-color lands, and what utility lands you might consider including.   • Wizards of the Coast Announces Pioneer Format!

    Published October 21, 2019 by Mrs. Mulligan

    As explained in their official blog post, Wizards of the Coast announced a new format today: Pioneer. Like Modern, the Pioneer format does not rotate, and includes cards that have been in standard since a certain set. But unlike Modern, it starts at Return to Ravnica, leaving a much smaller card pool to work with. What does this mean for your old Standard decks? How does mana work in this format?
